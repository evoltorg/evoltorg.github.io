\n

The Web via Email

\n

As any decent friend would do (;-)) I offered to help her out.

\n

If I send an email to: \n agora@dna.affrc.go.jp\n with no subject, and this in the body of the email :

\n
SEND http://news.google.com/news?q=swaziland \n
\n

After a while I receive this reply:

\n
\n This mail is not a spam but the automatic reply to your mail;
\n From: yourmail@yourmail.com
\n To: agora@dna.affrc.go.jp
\n Date: Mon, 1 Apr 2002 12:38:33 +0300
\n ---------------------------

After this I get the whole html results page from google news for the keyword: \n Swaziland in text form with all the hrefs below it, allowing me to make further \n requests if needed via email

\n

What I had basically done was, send the http://news.google.com \n search string to a mail robot, which browsed then download, then emailed the \n page back to me.

\n

What if I wanted not just a single page but also all the pages pointed to by \n that page?

\n

No problem, the agora service has a command called DEEP.

\n

For instance if I send: DEEP http://www.cnn.com, the service would send me the first page i.e. www.cnn.com and also all the \n content of the pages which link from cnn.com. There are quite a few powerful \n commands like this, which I will not go into. To get detailed info about all \n the agora services commands send an email to agora@dna.affrc.go.jp, with WWW \n in the body of the email.

\n

Other services

\n

My good lady friend used agora for a while, but she found a page referring \n to a pdf document on a web page that she wanted to see very much. Again she \n looked to me for help.

\n

The www4mail Service

\n

If I send an email to: www4mail@wm.ictp.trieste.it \n with this in the body:

\n
http://www.undp.org/dpa/publications/xyz.pdf
\n After a while I receive the file xyz.pdf back as a file by email.

\n

This service also sends back HTML files, if I sent the same google news search \n URL to the www4mail service, it sends me back the search results page as an \n HTML file.

\n
