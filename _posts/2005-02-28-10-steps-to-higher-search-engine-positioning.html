There is perhaps no more level playing field in business than the \n Internet. It is this fact that has created millionaires from paupers. \n The amount of money that can be made depends of course on your industry \n and your products and/or services but to be sure, if it can be sold \n at all, it can be sold online.

\n

While there are many methods out there for building a profitable \n website, from banner ads to email campaigns, by far the most cost \n effective over time has proven repeatedly to be search engine positioning. \n That major advantage search engine positioning has over other methods \n of producing revenue online is that once high rankings are attained \n and provided that the tactics used were ethical and that continued \n efforts are made to keep them, they can essentially hold and provide \n targeted traffic indefinitely. Your site will rise and your site \n may sometimes fall in the rankings but a solid and complete optimization \n of your site will insure that through algorithm changes you may \n fluctuate but you will not disappear.

\n

I have been ranking websites highly on the Internet for quite a \n few years now and there are some essential rules that, if followed, \n will insure that over time your website does well and holds solid \n and profitable positions on the major search engines.

\n

Here are the 10 steps to higher search engine positioning:

\n

Step One - Choosing Keywords
\n You first must choose your keywords. This is perhaps the most important \n step of the process as incorrectly targeting phrases can result \n in traffic that is not interested in your product. There are three \n tools that I use virtually every day to help pick the most appropriate \n keywords:

\n
    \n
  1. Overture's \n Search Term Suggestion Tool
    2. \n
  2. WordTracker
    3. \n
  3. A Brain
    4. \n
\n

The last in the list is the most important. Look through the potential \n keyword phrases and think, "Who would be searching using that phrase?" \n If the answer is, "a student looking for information" then chances \n are it won't result in a sale. If the answer is "Someone who is \n looking specifically for a product I offer," then obviously this \n is a prime candidate as a targeted keyword phrase.

\n

Step Two - Site Content
\n Even before I optimize websites I like to get a good deal of new \n content down in order to insure that I know exactly where I'm going \n and exactly what I need to do to get there. Creating some of the \n new content before starting the optimization process can be doubly \n helpful in that it can reveal potential additions to your website \n that you may not have considered (a forum or blog for example). \n If you already have a site, perhaps simply sit on your back deck, \n sip on a coffee and image what you would do if your whole site was \n lost and you had to start again (other than launch into a very colorful \n discussion with your hosting company).

\n

Step Three - Site Structure
\n A solid site structure is very important. Creating a site that is \n easily spidered by the search engines yet attractive to visitors \n can be a daunting and yet entirely rewarding endeavor. To adequately \n structure your website you must "think like a spider" which is not \n as difficult as it may sound. A search engine spider reads your \n web page like you would read a book. It starts at the top left, \n reads across, and then moves down.

\n

Priority must be given then, to what you place near the top of \n your page.

\n

Step Four - Optimization
\n Once you have your keyword targets, your content created and your \n site structure established you must now move on to the most obvious \n step, the optimization of your content.

\n

As noted above, a spider places importance on what it reads highest \n on the page and so beginning with a sentence that includes your \n targeted phrase only makes sense. That said, stuffing in keywords \n in hopes that it will add weight to your page generally doesn't \n work. The term "keyword density" refers to the percentage of your \n content that is made up of your targeted keywords. There are optimum \n densities according to many reputable SEO's though exactly what \n they are is debatable. Estimates seem to range anywhere from 4 \n or 5% to 10% to 12% (quite a gap isn't it).

\n

Personally, when it comes to keyword density I prescribe to one \n rule: put your keywords in the content as much as you can while \n keeping it comfortably readable to a human visitor.

\n

Some do it first, I do it last. Regardless of when you do it you \n must choose your heading. At the beginning of your content you have \n the opportunity to use the <h1> tag to specify the heading \n of your content. This tag is given extra weight and is also an indicator \n to the search engine of where your actual content starts. Make sure \n to use your keywords in the heading but don't shy away from also \n adding additional words (though not too many).

\n

Step Five - Internal Linking
\n To insure that your website gets fully indexed you have to make \n sure that the spiders have an easy path through your website. Text \n links make the best choice as the anchor text (the actual words \n used to link to a specific page) add relevancy to that page for \n the words used to link to it. For example, if I ran a website on \n acne and had a treatments page I could link to it with an image, \n with text reading "Click for more information on how to treat this \n skin condition" or simply "Acne Treatments". When a search engine \n spider hits an image it has no idea what the image is and, while \n it will follow the link, it will not give any weight to the page \n it hits. If you use text that does not contain the keywords you \n are targeting you are essentially supplying the engine with the \n same lack of relevancy as with an image, but if you use the phrase \n "Acne Treatments" to link to your acne treatments page you are attaching \n relevancy to that page for those keywords.

\n

There are two main ways to insure that your site gets well spidered \n AND that the relevancy is added. The first is to place text links \n on the bottom of your homepage to your main internal pages (not \n EVERY page, that just looks odd). The second is to create a sitemap \n to all your internal pages and link to it from your homepage. Both \n methods have advantages and disadvantages but that's a whole article \n unto itself.

\n

Step Six - Human Testing
\n So now you have your site, it's optimized and you have your navigation \n in place. The next step is to put it past someone who has never \n seen your site (and preferably who won't know how much work you've \n put in and tell you it's great even if it's not).

\n

Ask them to find specific information and see how long it takes. \n Ask someone else to just surf your site and watch which links they \n click and ask them why they chose those ones.

\n

Most importantly, find out how the content reads to them. You've \n spent hours working through the content at this point and are probably \n not the least biased on its readers. Find out how it reads to someone \n who has no invested interest in the site and correct any issues \n they may bring up.

\n

Step Seven - Submissions
\n I take a different philosophy than most when it cones to search \n engine submissions. I submit to directories (both general and topic-specific) \n and to a few topical search engines but for the most part I've found \n submitting to Google, Yahoo, MSN and the other major engines has \n proven to be a bit of a waste of time. The major search engines \n are spidering search engines, which means they will follow links \n to wherever they go. Simply having sites that are spidered by the \n major search engines linking to you will get your site found.

\n

When I have spent time submitting my sites I have found they get \n picked up in about a week. When I have simply skipped this step \n and sought out reputable directories and other sites to get links \n from I have found that at least the homepage of the site gets indexed \n in as little as two days.

\n

Neither will hurt your rankings but simply to make the best use \n of your time, seek our directories and other websites to get links \n from and leave the spiders to find you on their own.

\n

Step Eight - Link Building
\n All of the major search engines give credit to sites that have quality \n links pointing to them. How many is enough depends on your industry \n and targeted phrases. Running a search on Google the reads "link:www.yourcompetition.com" \n will reveal approximately how many links a competitor has.

\n

The first place to seek links is with general and topic-specific \n directories. After that you may want to move into reciprocal link \n building. Reciprocal link building is the exchange of links between \n two websites. Some webmasters will simply link to any website that \n links back to them. I highly recommend being more particular than \n that.

\n

Find websites that you believe your site visitors would genuinely \n be interested in and you've probably found a good link partner. \n You want to find links from sites that are related to yours.

\n

There are obviously many more methods to building links than directories \n and reciprocal link building. Again though, this is a whole article \n (or more) in itself.

\n

Step Nine - Monitoring
\n Whether you use WebPosition \n Gold or just run searches manually by hand you will have to \n monitor the major search engines for your targeted phrases. Also, \n you will need to review your stats to see where your traffic is \n coming from and what search terms are being used to find you.

\n

If a month passes and you don't see any changes then more work \n needs to be done. I'm certainly not stating that you should take \n a month off, a solid search engine positioning strategy involves \n constantly adding content, building links, and insuring that your \n visitors are getting the information they want to have and finding \n it as easily as possible.

\n

Step Ten - Reward Yourself
\n So you've done it. It's taken many, many hours of work but your \n rankings are doing well. What you've created is a solid position \n that will stand the tests of time provided that you continually \n revisit the above noted steps and insure that your website is always \n one step ahead of your competition (who have noticed you climbing \n and succeeding as you would notice others climbing up around your \n ranking).

\n

Now it's time to turn off your computer, take your partner out \n (you haven't had much time for them lately) and have a great week(end). \n You've got a lot of work to do to maintain and build on these rankings \n but the hardest part is over. Congratulations!
