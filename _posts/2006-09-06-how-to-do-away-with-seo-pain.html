The purpose of search engine optimization is to bring success to a website. Is it still possible to gain impressive site traffic and rank when almost all webmasters use the same web marketing techniques and strategies? The internet world calls for uniqueness and originality. This is what you are supposed to give it. But with the ever changing demands of the search engines, would there still be a painless SEO?

The Search Engine Craze



Search engine optimization is beneficial to most websites. It was even believed to be the ultimate key to the highest ranking on the search engine result pages. Search engine optimization was a painless task before. All you need are keywords incorporated on the Meta tags of your website and then you can rightly expect to be at the top of the search engine result pages!

However the search engines have changed their craze. It does this often especially when it comes to determining whether what site to place on the top of the SERPs. The Meta tags that once used to be the key for higher ranking have lost its charisma to the search engines. The trend has given favor to the doorway pages.

And then the search engines are not yet satisfied, they change their trend again. This time the demand was converted to links – more specifically incoming or inbound links. The more incoming links your site has, the more important your website appears on the eyes of the search engines. Webmasters began soliciting links because they are important for their website’s ranking. Along with the incoming links is the trend for paid links and ads.

Is There Still A Room for Painless SEO in the Internet?



With all these changes in the search engines’ craze and with all these unending cycle of trends in determining a website’s importance, can we still expect for a painless search engine optimization for our websites? Search engine optimization still does work! And good news to all webmasters, SEO can still be a painless task. The key to painless SEO lies on the capability of the webmasters to balance all elements that are considered relevant by the search engines.

Don’t you think it is time that we think of improving our site naturally than following every trend of the search engines? It will be a painful task to follow every change of the search engines. Getting a higher rank for your website is as simple as making your website relevant and legitimate.

The internet competition requires you to update your website continuously to maintain its importance and originality. SEO is also as simple as balancing the keywords of your website. Moreover, search engine optimization is about relevant content. Don’t mind the change of trends of the search engines. As long as your website has relevant content, it won’t be affected much by the changes!
