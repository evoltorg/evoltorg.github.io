Should you buy this?

\n
    \n
  • If you're low on money and not a usability freak, just read the \n online chapter. The rest isn't as good.
    • \n
  • If you're ok on money, get it!
    • \n
  • If you need to convince co-workers about usability (it's really good for \n visual people), definitely get it.
    • \n
  • If you're doubting between Jakob's book and this, get this and print out \n Jakob's alertboxes. (his book is really just rehashing his alertboxes)
    • \n
\n

Now that we have the basics settled, what is so good about this book?

\n
    \n
  • It really helps you take a fresh look, away from all the holy wars, \n on how people really use the web.
    • \n
  • It is funny and entertaining, which for a book on usability is a \n Great Achievement.
    • \n
  • Our visual guy (from a print background) really liked it.
    • \n
  • I liked the first chapters better than the later ones, but they are all \n worth reading.
    • \n
