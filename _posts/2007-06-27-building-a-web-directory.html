I will write about \nall the aspects, about building up a directory, promote it, creating link partners and link exchanges, the tools I use, blending into the market, \nacting in new directions, the money I make with this hobby, the time I spend, and everything else related to this.

First thing is first, so let's start with the beginning.


1. The name.

\n

There are around 10000 directories on web, or maybe even more, if we count the link-exchange directories most of the websites these days have. \nMany are very old, powerful, with huge traffic, very good advertised and have lots of people behind them. Many offer things you can't even imagine: back \nmassages, millions of $ in your bank account, trips around the world and much more... Just joking...\nNow, seriously, you need an original name. "Free Web Directory" is already taken, so think \ndeeper. Let's take, for example, my directory, My Green Corner. Well, it definitely has originality. It sounds good, it's one of a kind, and I \ncould go on with all the hype. But here are the bad things: 3 words, very long domain name and not quite a directory name as well. Hmm, the first \nletter is very, very bad chosen too. In front of "m" comes "a", "b" and so on, and many guys out here see things alphabetically. But what could \nbe a good directory name? From my personal experience, I think it should have the word "directory" in it. And it should start with "a". Or "1", \nalthough numbers are not always a good idea. And it should be an easy to remember name. For example, "Aviva directory". A good directory name, \nwith a lot of advertising and money spent around it, equals a successful directory. But what if you don't have money? I didn't had any money when \nI started this, and I assume you don't either. Or maybe you have the money, but you are not risking them. So if advertising comes out of the question, \na good named should come in. Go fishing, do some thing inspiring, or just lay in your bed for a few days and think about your directory name. \nThese day are the most important for your directory. You may also want to take a look at Google trends, or at the zeitgeist list, if you want to see what are the most searched words on web for free.

\n

For my directory, I have spent a week at the mountain. When I sat down on the chair, ready to chose my directory's name, I was seeing green \neverywhere, so the "MGC" choice was normal.





2. The hosting.

\n

You have found a million dollar name, and now, you want to build a site. If you didn't knew until now, sites are not floating on air, they \nare hosted on some little (or big, depending on preferences) black boxes we call servers. Most of these servers have an operating system. Some of \nthem have chosen Linux because it's free, some of them have chosen Windows because is "nice". Well, here comes the good part: you will take the Linux one. \nWhy? Because most of the open source applications have been developed for Linux. You know how they say that best things in life are free, right?

\n

Now, about the money again, there are 2 types of hosting: free, and paid. If this is your first site, you can definitely settle down for the free one. \nThere are many free hosts that don't require a banner on your page, just type "free hosting" on Google and you're done. Look at some features, see if php \nis enabled, if they provide mysql. Look at the bandwidth, see if they provide around 2Gbs, and if they are, you have found your hosting. But, because \nalmost all free web hosing is not so reliable, doesn't offer 24/7 customer support, doesn't have php enabled, has different bandwidth and processor load \nrestrictions, I recommend you choose the paid one. For somewhere between $50 and $150, many, many hosting companies offer good uptime, and almost all the \nfeatures you need to build your directory. Choose a good name, or ask around on some forums about hosting.

\n

I am now hosting my site on Godaddy. I got here after a lot of asking and looking, but I can say I am definitely \nsatisfied, until now. The customer support is very good, I always got feedback in a few hours after posting my questions. And believe me, I had a lot. \nOn this plan, called deluxe, I can virtually host as many sites as I want. This is a great opportunity for a low budget start, because I only paid around \n80$/year. But if you have more advanced needs and know your way with apache, you may want to think about getting a more professional hosting.

\n

Tip: always search the hosting before buying the domain. Most of the times, you will host you domain from where you have bought it, with the same \ncompany.

3. The script.

\n

There are many directory scripts out there. What I am using on My Green Corner is a script named phpld, from PhpLinkDirectory. It is the most used \nscript for directories. You can download the latest phpld free version using this link: \nhttp://www.phplinkdirectory.com/phpLD-2.1.2.zip or you can navigate to \nphpld site and buy the last version. Other scripts like esyndicat, or site-sift, are far behind in terms \nof functionality, templates available, features, support and feedback, and online community in general. Take it from here or buy it, read the \ninstructions, and then install it on your server.

\n

Here is how you do that. First, you will have to create a database on your hosting account. You can do that in your admin cpanel. After that, \nyou can use a ftp program to upload your files. I usually use Total Commander. Type "download Total Commander" in Google if you don't have it already, \ndownload it, install it, open it and then press "CTRL+F" to open the connection manager. Use the hosting ftp username and password, and create a connection.\n Connect to your server and upload your files. Then follow the installation instructions.

\n

Why am I using this phpld? Because of many reasons: I found great support on phpld forum, the admin interface is really nice and simple, the script's \ncode itself is very easy to understand, and, with little coding skills, you can transform it in whatever you would like, and I found many good templates \nfor it. \nWhat are templates? Well, at first, I didn't knew either, and if you ask yourself this question, you definitely need to read the things below. Templates \nare files that you add to your directory, so that you can make it look nicer. There are many places where you could find phpld templates, and one is \nvisiting the free phpld templates page on My Green Corner. You can find many other free \ntemplates on forums, on other directories and so on. They important thing is to take the template that matches your directory script's version, and to \nfollow the steps that are written in the text file inside the zip archive. Install a contact us page too, you will definitely need it. You will also need \na logo and a nice and original banner. There are a lot of free programs on web in which you can build a logo. Anyway, the best remains Photoshop. If you \nare a beginner with Photoshop, type "Photoshop tutorials" on Google, and spend some days learning the basics. And another place where you would like to look for answers, and where i found a lot,\nis W3Schools, a site with many tutorials for anything that interests you.

5. Add quality.

\n

Well, you have advertised your directory, and now, you have some links pending. Approve them very fast, to please your subscribers. You can use \nthis tool I got from a friend of mine that owns \nPegasus Directory to approve as many listings as you want, very fast. Just make sure that they are quality links, \nlinks that have good and unique content, that provide a real service to web surfers, and that are really useful. The only thing important is the quality of\n your web directory. Gambling links should be in the Gambling category, Loans in the Financial one, and so one. Want my advice? Do not accept links on your\n main categories. Do not accept reciprocal links from pages that look like a link farm. Link farms are not well seen by the Search engines, because they\n where used in the past to manipulate their ranking algorithms. And do not accept inner pages. These tend to disappear with time, as webmasters modify the\n structure of their site.

Update you site daily. Thus, you will be very loved by search engines. They like new stuff, and they will keep crawling new information on your website,\n if you will give it to them.

\n

Because your directory is most of the time used by webmasters, you can add some \nfree webmaster tools, like the ones you see on My Green Corner. I have also used some web tools \nfrom Iwebtools.

6. Links good to eat.

\n

There are 2 kinds of links: "good" and "not so good". I will explain what are good links. "Not so good" links are all the others. Good links are links \nthat come from sites related to yours. For example, other directories, seo companies, and so on. It is like this because of the growing complexity of \nGoogle and other search engine sorting algorithms. So, if you find your link on a page of site that has the main subject related to a directory, that's a \ngood thing. Good links are also links from sites that have no connection with yours, but the page from where they are linking you has many other links to \nsimilar sites, meaning links to other directories. Great links are .edu and .gov. These are most trusted by search engines, because they have a \nnon-economical value, but are also the most hard to get.

\n

If you find good sites, don't hesitate to contact their webmaster and to ask polite for a link exchange.
