\n I recently started a project based on 'Virtual Communities' \n . As I was conducting research and benchmarking, \n my challenge stuck me - \n

\n

\n "How do I build a virtual community based around a specific \n geographic area?"\n

\n

\n It is easy to see how communities based on a topical interest are\n formed. With a deep interest and passion for a \n topic of choice, such as politics or hobbies, people quickly \n find a 'common interest' and begin to share.\n

\n

\n Geographic based communities are of a different nature.\n People are less reliant on local community than previous \n generations - most government and important services are available \n regardless of your locality.\n

\n

\n I don't have the strategy yet, but I am sure that online community \n services that improve quality of life are key. I'll share \n the results of the project in Feb. 2002. In the meantime. here is \n a collection of useful virtual community resources;\n
