\nYou've heard about it -- maybe even read the warnings. It's too late for second-guessing now. It's do or die time.\n

\n

\nAn article, Sites for the Blind published by The Standard, has some chilling news for those responsible for U.S. federal government web sites. The deadline to be compliant is June 21, 2001.\n

\n

Specifically, regulations for Web site accessability state, "Directions and cues for electronic forms must be readable with tools such as screen readers; all information conveyed with color must be available without color; users must be alerted to timed responses and given a chance to signal they need more time to complete a function; pictures and graphics must be tagged with text descriptions."\n

\n

\nYou may find helpful an article entitled, Making Your Web Site Accessible to the Blind, written by Curtis Chong, Director of Technology, National Federation of the Blind. \n

Be sure you read the other accessibility articles on evolt.org.
